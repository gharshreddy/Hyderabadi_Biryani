Hyderabadi Biryani

Introduction

Hyderabadi Biryani is a staple part of the Indian Cuisine. It is made with marinated meat with spiced steamed basmathi rice. Typical biryani is made with chicken or mutton. But there is a vegan version of it as well, made with spiced veggies. Hyderabadi biryani is generally believed to be originated as a blend of Mughlai and Iranian cuisine in the kitchens of the Nizam rulers of the historic Hyderabad State. It is a common dish made during the Ramzan season in Muslim households. But it is equally enjoyed and celebrated as the best spiced rice dish by everyone, particularly non-vegitarians.

Ingredients

  • 1 kg meat
  • 1 pack Hyderbadi Biryani Masala
  • 1 Tbsp salt
  • 1 Tbsp ginger garlic paste
  • 1 Tbsp red chilli paste
  • 1 Tbsp green chilli paste
  • 1/2 Tbsp cardamom powder
  • 3-4 sticks cinnamon
  • 1 Tbsp cumin seeds
  • 4 cloves pinch of mace mint leaves
  • 2 Tbsp lemon juice
  • 1250 gm curd
  • 4 Tbsp clarified butter
  • 750 gm semi cooked Basmathi rice
  • 1 tsp saffron
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/2 cup of oil
  • Garnish Ingredients
  • boiled eggs
  • carrots,sliced
  • cucumbers

Instructions

  1. Marinate the meat in curd and Hyderbadi Biryani spice mix over night.
  2. Take the marinated meat. Then in a pan add meat, salt, ginger garlic paste, red chilli powder, green chilli paste, sauteed brown onions, cardamom powder, cinnamon, cumin seeds, cloves, mace, mint leaves and lemon juice. Mix it well.
  3. Add curd, clarified butter, semi cooked basmathi rice, saffron, water and oil and mix it well.
  4. Now apply sticky dough on the sides of the pan.
  5. Cover with lid to seal it and cook for about 25 minutes.
  6. Hyderabadi Biryani is ready to eat. Garnish it with boiled eggs, sliced carrots, cucumbers and serve hot.
